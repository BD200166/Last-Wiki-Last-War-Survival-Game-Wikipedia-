Daily Stuff and Alliance Duel

Radar Missions and Stacking

On the odd numbered days of Alliance Duel, your earn a great deal of points from
completing radar missions. For this reason, it's very effective to stack radar missions on the days when
they don't earn Duel points. To stack radar misssions, you complete the activity but DON'T COLLECT it once done.
You leave it on the screen with its red dot. That will prevent it from expiring. To avoid shifting your time, try to
stack at 1 less than max (eg 34/35). If you do want to shift your refresh time, let it stack to max (eg 35/35) and collect
one radar mission at the time you want to set as refresh. See linked post for graphic about that.

You can send 4 trucks daily (by default), and you can refresh a truck with Trade Contracts that you earn
from various sources. Keep 40 trade contracts in stock so that you can refresh all 4 trucks to UR on
days 2 & 6 of Alliance Duel. You can send a certain number of Secret Mobile Team explorations each day
(number depending on level). You can refresh the whole list of unstarted missions using Secret Orders that
you earn from various sources. However, it's best to save Secret Orders to refresh on Days 2 & 6 of Alliance Duel
until you get between 4 and 6 gold secret missions. Make sure to start the gold missions before refreshing the list.
To aid ally secret mission, it's as simple as going to second tab and aiding them when their exploration is complete.
To loot other explorations, you have to look for them on the world map manually, which you can only see at certain
zoom levels.

Last note about Duel. All the smart players save their hero shards and skill medals to use only on Day 4 of Duel each week.
You're here reading a guide, so you're a smart player too!

PvP

First, let's talk about Base Defense. You should only try to defend if your main squad can survive in the first attack or
second successive attack (i.e., the attacker is around your level and spending habits).

If you can't defend or can't be online to make that determination, DISABLE your defenses.
How to enable/disable defenses. Go to the Wall in your base, click the House+ icon and uncheck the squads. Any time
you unlock a new squad, make sure to uncheck them too. Sometimes it makes sense to have zero defense, and sometimes it
makes sense to have main squad as defense, but having your weaker squads participate in defense is the biggest mistake
you can make.

Why this works: Let's say a much stronger player attacks you 5 times (assuming you're level 20 with 3 squads).
You would lose about 10k to 12k troops in those 5 attacks if you had 3 defense teams. If you had only a single
defense (strongest squad), you'd lose about 4k to 5k troops. If you had no defense at all, you'd lose about 1k troops (due to
breach attack, which doesn't count as kills for attacker). It's a huge difference.

Attacking bases serves two purposes in this game: (1) to plunder resources and (2) to earn points during Kill Events, such as
Enemy Buster (Day 6 of Duel)

Plundering gives the best resource to stamina ratio in the game, but be mindfull of defenses/retaliation, your current plunder
limits, etc. There's a separate guide that goes more into it (⁠tips-strategies ⁠Attacking Commanders to Gain Re…)
When attacking other bases for Kill Events, note that only battles can give kills for points. So, someone with no defense
or no troops won't earn you any event points or increase your individual kill count. That's the other reason for disabling your
own defenses, which is to prevent your opponents from earning points from attacking you.

PvP cont'd

You can attack bases far from you without giving them too much warning by redirecting your squad to attack at the last moment.
Furthermore, your primary squad marches about 3 times faster (if garage 1 researched far enough) to resources than they do to a base
or zombie. That means instead of marching to a base for 1 minute (giving them and their alliance warning), you can do a 20 second
march and sneak attack.

Pay attention to the power rating of your opponent as a way to predict if they have troops in base. You'll eventually get a feel for
how to predict if they have 0 troops, a few thousand troops, or a full drill ground. Examine the battle report after your first attack.
It is an absolute waste to use your secondary squads to land an attack on an opponent's base because (A) your weaker squads have a greater
chance of failing and (B) they have a lower load capacity for plundering. You can use your second squad as a feint attack, but withdraw
before it lands (if you're trying to draw your opponent into attacking you when thinking your base is only weakly defended)

Lastly, zeroing bases is something the developers want you to do because it encourages spending. However, it's actually counterproductive
in most situations. There are several reasons why it's not good to zero bases, but instead I'll state when it is productive: (1) you need
that base gone such as a strong attacker whose squad is out marching, (2) earning points in Zonewar Duel and it's an enemy server base

Team Composition

First, for every squad, put 2 Defenders (shield icons) in the front row and 3 Attackers in back row. This is due to the mechanics of battle.
Every character has an auto attack aka basic attack, a fatal skill, an upgradable passive, and a 4-star passive.

What makes an attacker good in their role is a fatal skill that deals damage (even better if it has secondary effect like targeting back
row or causing buff/debuff) and a damage boosting passive. Read Stetmann's skills to see why he is currently one of the best attackers to have
(this is not an endorsement to purchase him).

What makes a defender good in their role is a fatal skill and passive that increases their own or the whole team's survivability.
Read Carlie's skills to see why she's such a great defender (not the best because she is niche but still great). In the case of
Carlie, it's not just her skills, but it's also because she is Aircraft (and most players use tanks). Aircraft faction receives
only 80% damage from Tank Faction. In the same Rock/Paper/Scissors mechanics of the game, Tank Faction receives only 80% damage from
Artillery Faction, and Artillery Faction receives only 80% damage from Aircraft Faction.

UR heroes are generally better than SSR heroes but only when they don't trail too far behind in the total number of stars and levels.
So, your newly acquired 0 star DVA shouldn't be replacing any 2-5 star SSR, but if you get her to 3 stars, have her replace any 5 star SSR attacker.

Characters receive a MASSIVE buff in performance when reaching 4 stars, so prioritize getting everyone
on your team to that before going too heavy into reaching 5 stars.

Team Composition cont'd

Gear allocation is something I see many many players handle ineffectively.

When equipping and leveling gear, you'll actually want to prioritize defense gear (i.e. gold armor and gold radar) for your
defenders and attack gear (gold gun and gold data chip) for your attackers. That means the very last thing you would do is equip
a gold gun on Murphy and level it, but 90-95% of players do this very early not realizing how ineffective it is.

After you've gotten your prioritized gear sorted, you can work on putting some gold defense gear on your attackers, then gold data
chips on your defenders (to level 20 for damage resistance and extra HP), and lastly gold guns on your defenders (for a little extra defense).

Marshall is a support character rather than an explicit attacker and defender, but between those two roles, he actually does better as a
defender in the front row if you don't have two UR defenders sufficiently tiered.

Alliance Exercise - Marshal Guard

An alliance with a whale account can knock out the damage requirements quickly, but for everyone else, a sound strategy is needed
to do the most damage possible.

To explain this strategy, I'm going to use an example of there being 10 members online and ready to go when you start the exercise.

Each member starts a rally in about 30 second intervals. It's important to not start them all at the same time. You only have to think
about this for the first 4-5 minutes to establish a good rhythm.
The rallies are led by each member's second squad. The reason is to make the main squad free to join any rally as often as possible.
Everyone joins the shortest available rally with their main squad. When the rhythm is right, you end up attacking about every 2-3 minutes.
If there are open spots, don't worry. When there are 30 seconds remaining to a Marshal Guard rally, it will auto fill with the strongest
squads available (including offline members).
The two biggest obstacles to doing this right are (A) members starting too many rallies at the same time, breaking the rhythm and reducing
how many times you can attack with main squad, and (B) members joining rallies with their weak teams, which takes up spots that could have
otherwise been filled with a team that can actually do damage.

Note that at time of this posting, your damage done decreases with each increasing level of alliance guard. For simplicity, let's just say it
decreases by half with each level. So, the difficulty in obtaining max rewards increases exponentially with level.

Troop/Unit Training

The most optimal troop training strategy is to train them in 3 stages. This doesn't make training any faster or use any fewer resources, but it
allows you to increase your point scoring during relevant events such as unit training in Arms Race and Duel's Day 5 Activity.

Lets say TX=max level of troop you can train, TY=one level below max, and TZ=two levels below max. The strategy is to train TZ all throughout
the week (assuming you already have enough TX for all your squads). During Unit Training of Arms Race, promote TZ—>TY and/or TY—>TX (possible to
do two rounds in each barracks without speedups during that phase). During Day 5 of Duel, you would promote all or nearly all TY/TZ to TX throughout
the day before going into Desert Storm Battlefield or Day 6 of Duel. Then, once you're done with Kill Events, resume with restocking TZ.

As an example, if you are HQ 25, then TX would be level 8 units (requiring lv24 barracks), TY would be lv 7 units (requiring lv20 barracks), and TZ
would be lv 6 units (requiring lv17 barracks)

To make use of this strategy, you need to keep some barracks at lower levels than the max possible. Out of 4 barracks, I suggest keeping 2 that can
train to TX and 2 that can only train up to TY. This is because promoting troops automatically goes to max level for that barracks.
When healing from the hospital, it's usually better to repair in smaller doses rather than all at the same time. Note that although you have
multiple hospital buildings in your base, they all basically function as a single hospital.
Plan space in drill grounds around being able to train 2k new troops daily (estimate based on 4 barracks).

Auto Rally

Automatically enabled for everyone in the alliance once you've finished the one-time alliance research. Works for boomers - open spots
automatically fill after 40 seconds have passed on rally timer, drawing the weakest squad and 5 units from any member. Works for
Marshal Guard — open spots automatically fill when 30 seconds remain on rally timer with strongest available squad and full units.

Zombie Invasion

The first error people make when encountering Zombie Invasion for the first time is they add all the combat power in the rally and think
it will work if it's greater than the recommended power. That's not a reliable way to determine it.
You'll want to have someone close to the recommended power leading the rally, and then weaker members can join later.

Don't base it on summed power. Need to have each individual with about 60-80% of the recommended power (this is not exact). You'll get
a feel for what you need to take out each level of zombie boss as the event goes on

As an example, 5 players with 1.6M power will get wiped in all 5 rounds against a level 5 without making a dent.
However, 2 players with 4M power could almost take it depending on how your teams are built. Lead with the strongest
and have the weaker players join last for potential cleanup (when the rounds get easier). Also, everyone should be
sending only their main team unless the ones who joined are already guaranteed to win and you're just filling the rally to launch.

Desert Storm Battlefield

Because true matchmaking doesn't exist yet for DSB, I won't write up a comprehensive guide. Right now, most opponents are selected as
developers pick winners and losers creating massively unbalanced matches, so nothing you do will change which side wins.

However, there is still the aspect of earning individual points for a separate section of rewards for participants . If there is no
opponent to meet you in battle, it is currently impossible to get 200k individual points with only 3 squads.

When you have uneven matches (one side greatly outnumbers their opponent or greatly overpowers their opponent with similar number of participants)
it's actually not a very good strategy to attack bases. This is because successful base attacks cause units to perish: those units don't earn points
for anyone, and they are gone from battlefield forever. You're basically throwing points away. So, there are two ways to increase individual points
for everyone (both sides) in uneven matches

Keep all battles in structures or at the oil fields.
Continuously change structure ownership to create structure breach points (boxes that have to be collected with scout planes).
Coordination between your alliance and opponent alliance

If/when they fix this to be a balanced mode, all that changes because then there's an actual battle with numerous strategies available.

City Capture - an addendum

You can hold only 6 cities regardless of level. The Capitol is not considered part of this limit (i.e., 7 territories if you also hold Capitol)
You can declare war on only 3 cities each day. You can also only declare war on one city at a time, which will last until the city
is captured or the 12 h timer expires.

You can repeat march when attacking a city similar to something you do when plundering a dead base
You can release cities by way of an abandonment process that takes one hour.
The buffs of gold/food/iron output are the most useless buffs. Gathering bonus speed is good for events particularly activity points
for day 1 of Duel. The best buffs are for construction and research speed.

If your alliance is the top alliance, it's useful to share occupation of level 6 cities so that it's easy to get honor points every
time there is a Capitol War. Closing off access to the Capitol makes it difficult for all alliance members to get max honor points, thus
losing out on potential rewards. Not impossible though, you would just have to organize battles with other alliances' members in the farms
of the gray zone, or make sure your alliance members withdraw from Capitol as soon as they reach max individual rewards due to the reinforcement limit.

Structure Upgrade Plan in Base

HQ has two prerequisites for each level, one of them always being the Tech Center, and the other rotating between Hospital, Drill Ground,
Barracks, Tank Center, Wall, and Alliance Center.

The biggest reason anyone has a food shortage or an iron shortage is not because they don't get enough food or iron respectively, it's because
they upgrade their structures in a manner that constantly drains one resource more than the other. To keep your resource consumption about
even, plan your upgrades in pairs or trios (whether you do one after the other or have purchased construction slots to do them simultaneously).
This is the scheme I recommend:

Keep 2 Drill Grounds at same level as Wall
Keep 1 Hospital at same level as Tank Center (2nd hospital same level as Aircraft center, and 3rd hospital same level as Artillery Center)
Alliance Center can be paired with Gear Factory (until Alliance Center is 23 and Gear Factory is 20)

Structures to keep at Max level or near max level:

Training Bases (until they are at 25)
Smelters (0-4 levels below HQ, also slowing down around mid 20s)
Material Workshops (2-6 levels below HQ, also slowing down around early to mid 20s)
2 Barracks (see deeper strategy in Troop/Unit Training section)

Structures you can start ignoring early:

Fields and Mines. Stop somewhere between 15 and 20. Upgrading these higher than that starts to have a null or negative effect. (Tarna here I stopped at level 18)
Warehouses - these are essentially useless
Builder Hut & Tavern - only upgrade as needed, but the benefit drops off very quickly.
